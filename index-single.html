The Challenge

Mental Illness is a National Crisis

The United States faces a mental health epidemic that disproportionately impacts medically underserved and rural communities. Too many people are slipping through the cracks of a system that wasn't designed for them.

In the Inland Empire and communities like it across California, the shortage of primary care providers means that getting help often feels impossible — even for those actively seeking it.

Navigating insurance, finding providers, understanding medications, and accessing support all require time and resources that many people simply don't have. MindConnect was built to bridge every one of those gaps.

Our Story

Empowering Communities Through Connection and Care

MindConnect was founded in the Inland Empire with a simple but urgent belief: every person deserves access to mental health support, regardless of their income, language, or zip code.

We started locally. We think nationally. And we work every day to connect people to the resources, support, and community they need to thrive.

Our Origin

Born from the Inland Empire

Founded by community members who lived through the gaps in mental health care, MindConnect is built on firsthand understanding of what underserved communities need.

The Problem We Couldn't Ignore

The Inland Empire — like many regions across the United States — faces a profound shortage of mental health resources. With only 35 primary care providers per 100,000 residents, and more than 500,000 people in the region affected by mental health conditions, the gap between need and access is staggering.

Our founders saw firsthand how families struggled: long wait times, confusing insurance requirements, language barriers, and the simple fear of not knowing where to start. Something had to change.

"We kept hearing the same story — 'I knew I needed help, I just didn't know how to get it.' That became our mission: be the bridge."

Why We Built MindConnect

MindConnect was created to solve a coordination problem. The resources often exist — the free clinics, the psychiatrists accepting new patients, the pharmacies with assistance programs. What's missing is the connective tissue that helps people find and access them.

We step into that role. We map the landscape of available care, build relationships with providers who share our values, and guide individuals through the process from first contact to ongoing support.

What Makes Us Different

Unlike traditional healthcare navigators, MindConnect provides ongoing peer support, not just referrals. We stay with you. We check in. We adapt to your changing needs. And we do it in both English and Spanish.
